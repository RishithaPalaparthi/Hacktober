Start local, End global

Posted by Admin on October 1, 2020

What do you do when you get an assignment? Apart from procrastinating, we all do one other common thing.
We GOOGLEEEE ITT!!

Giants like Google, Tesla and Apple work with over millions of lines of code. It's not possible for a few individuals to build something of that scale.

On the other hand, Android has under 1600 employees; Whereas linux, the os that powers over a billion systems, started as a personal project with his garage as his headquarters even today.

How did this work out?

The answer is...they turned towards OPEN SOURCE.

Ok wait... so, now What exactly open source?

As the name suggests, open source is letting the source code of a project be open to everyone. Well, HOW DOES THIS HELPP? Many developers like us, who love freeware contribute to the code base and it's development.

Not satisfied? We aren't either.

Let's have a look at a few established open source projects that we all know:


*ANDROID* *CHROMIUM* *PYTHON* *FIREFOX*


Hacktober?? What does that have to do with this?

Hacktoberfest is a global annual event that celebrates open source throughout the month of october.
It is open to everyone, irrespective of your occupation/role, proficiency with a language or coding as a whole.

"The power of open source is the power of the people. The people rule."

This year(2020), if you successfully submit 4 or more valid pull requests, you can either choose to get a tee or to plant a tree in the name of open source.

PRs(Pull Requests) can't be made to any public repository on GitHub, only the ones with their issues labeled hacktoberfest are considered this year.

Why we love it.

Networking with like minded people from all over the world, many fun events with goodies, T-shirts or even trees :P, all of this for *FREE*.

Jokes aside, we get to test, learn and contribute to new technologies that have the potential to make our lives easier, and it goes without saying, contributing to open source would be the perfect icing to your resume.

To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.
